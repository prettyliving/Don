A Poem for Don

I met you before I knew what staying meant. Before memory had a spine. Before I could name the way a life can be held together by someone who simply doesn’t leave. You didn’t arrive with a title. No script. No obligation written in blood. Just hands that showed up. Again. And again. And again like it was the easiest decision in the world. You made ordinary days feel chosen. Visits weren’t obligations; they were events. Time slowed down when you were there as if the world knew this was something worth pausing for. You never announced yourself as anything. You just were. Steady. Present. A constant in a life that could’ve tilted but didn’t because you stood where balance was needed. I’m twenty now. Old enough to understand that love isn’t noise. It’s consistency. It’s the quiet courage of always showing up even when no one is keeping score. So thank you for choosing me without ever having to say you did. For being proof that family is built one kept promise at a time. And if I don’t say it enough know this I saw you. I still do.

— Alicia

Some things don’t need fixing.
